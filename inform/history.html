A Short History from 1666 to 1993 and Beyond

  • Gutenberg, mid-15th century, from Mainz, developing printing press in Strasbourg.
  • 1666 - foundation of the Philosophical Transactions and the Journal des Scavants, the scientific journal as we know it today.
  • 1993 - some milestones.
    • 1990: Archie developed by McGill University School of Computer Science.
    • 1991: CERN (Tim Berners-Lee) releases the first Web server.
    • 1991: WAIS (wide area information servers, Brewster Kahle) released by Thinking Machines Corp.
    • 1991: Gopher released by University of Minnesota (Paul Lindner and Mark McCahill) - a campus-wide document delivery system.
    • 1992: Veronica, a search service for Gopher released by University of Nevada.
    • 1992: Berners-Lee: "the World-Wide Web is a year too late - Gopher has won".
    • February 1993: University of Minnesota introduces licensing for Gopher.

    • March 1993: Marc Andreessen at University of Illinois releases Mosaic browser.

    • July 1993: (Why not mention it?) Publication of Intelligent Information Retrieval : The Case of Astronomy and Related Space Science, A. Heck and F. Murtagh, Eds., Kluwer. (Check it out on amazon.com.) Completed approx. late 1992.
      Chapters in section entitled "Wide Area Network Resource Discovery Tools" by:
      A. Emtage, Archie; J. Fullton, WAIS; F. Anklesaria and M. McCahill, Gopher; and B. White, WWW.
    • July 1994: Development of WWW moves from CERN to the W3 Consortium.
    • October 1994: Netscape releases the Navigator browser.
    • November 1995: Microsoft releases the Internet Explorer browser.
