ERS image - SAR, Synthetic Aperture Radar

A SAR image taken by the European Space Agency's ERS satellite (ERS-1 or -2) is shown on the left. It contains a lot of what is termed speckle, - the radar image is deflected by foliage, soil, water, buildings. Note the "sparkle" half way up towards the right - this is caused by some reasonably large area more or less exactly reflecting back the radar signal. The second image is a filtered version of this image. It could be used to much more easily pick out and demarcate areas of similar trees, or of urban areas, for instance.

Commands used

mr_filter -m 4 -t 10 in.fits out.fits
This entails use of (i) a multiplicative noise model for the filtering, based on (ii) a pyramidal median transform.

Another command is mr_rfilter with options for noise modeling being Rayleigh, log-transformed Rayleigh and square-transformed Rayleigh.
